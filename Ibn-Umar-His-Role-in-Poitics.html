Ibn Umar: His Role in Politics

By: Dr. Omar Suleiman

Uthman tried to make Ibn Umar a judge

Uthman said do you disobey me

Ibn Umar said there are three types of judges

  1. Those woh are unjust
  2. Those who are going to hell
  3. Those who are going to heaven

Ibn Umar said I will not judge

Uthman said your father was a judge

Ibn Umar said my father asked the Prophet then the Prophet asked Jibril

Ibn Umar said who can I ask

Ibn Umar narrates the Prophet said leadership is a trust and a regret

Uthman excused Ibn Umar and said not to share it with the people then he will not find a judge

Ibn Umar is not in any army with Ali or Muawwiah

Ibn Umar defended and protected the sahaba

A man came and said to Ibn Umar you know Uthman did not fight in Badr

Ibn Umar said yes and the man said Uthman ran away from the battle of Uhud

Ibn Umar said yes and the man said how do you let a hypocrite lead us

Ibn Umar said as for Uhud Allah forgave all of them

As for Badr the Prophet told him to stay back because the Prophets daughter was sick and passed away then

The Prophet gave Uthman a share from the spoils of war

As for the bayah under the tree it was for avenging Uthman

When Uthman was killed the Sahaba offered Ibn Umar to be Khalifah

Ibn Umar had refused to be the Khalifah

Ibn Umar escaped from Madinah and Ali was upset over this

Ibn Umar left Ali a letter and he said I am not leaving only to protect myself

Ibn Umar told Hafsa to stay with him and do not be part of the politics and to be dafe from the fitna

Ibn Umar stays silent

Ibn Umar pretended that nothing was going on

Ali offered Ibn Umar to be governor of Sham

Ibn Umar said no and please excuse me

Ibn Umar got Hafsa to intercede on his behalf

Ali did not take it and that's when he left and sent Ali a letter

Ibn Umar made dua nothing keeps me from competing with people except fear of Allah

Ibn Umar hugged and kissed Hussein and tries to talk him out of going to Kufa

Ibn Umar did not want anyone to die for him so he takes out of politics
